The Designs

The Drowsy Chaperone - Musical

Pre production design sketches and descriptions

My designs were based around creating a 1940's New York apartment for a lonely man obsessed with musical theatre. The setting is an example of a modified Unit set. I used the apartment as the primary location. During the musical, each section of the apartment (i.e., the kitchen, dressing area, and bedroom/bookcase became a separate performance space when needed. The props used on the set were essential to the apartment's aesthetics; however, they also had to be functional as secondary props to the story within a story. The standard new york loft style of the design matched the character's personality and the general theme of the production.

Post production photographs and evaluation

Evaluation of Designs: Using the Principles of Art The unifying theme of the design - Musical Theatre obsessed, lonely, and overdramatic. Balance - Like the main character himself. The design was very eclectic and off-balance, showcasing the musicals out of control and whimsical theme. Alignment- Although the set was out of balance, the organization of the apartment was immaculate and detailed. Emphasis - The design focused on the main character's red chair on the stage right side of the design. This design choice allowed the audience's attention to stay on the main character even when other things were happening on stage. Lighting was also used to direct the audience's focus and was very effective in changing locations quickly during the production. Movement - Matching the quirkiness of the staging, the movement on the set was free-flowing. Pattern - Simple shapes were used throughout the design to match the main character's style and the broadway musical theme of the show. Contrast - The design element that was the most different was the dreamlike structure that entered the apartment center stage during the end of the 1st Act. This was a flower-laced garden tower that allowed multiple levels and action to happen within the apartment.
