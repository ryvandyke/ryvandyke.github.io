The Designs

Anything Goes - Musical

Pre production design sketches and descriptions

Description: My designs were based around creating a 1920's passenger ship. The setting is an example of a Unit set. Using the ship's center as a separate performance space, I created an interior space such as a stateroom and a nightclub. The props used on the set matched the motif of the time period and were designed to be functional yet lightweight and moveable by performers. The art deco-inspired style of the designs matched the characters' personalities and the general theme of the production.

Post production photographs and evaluation

Evaluation of Designs: Using the Principles of Art The unifying theme of the design - Art deco style and 1920s overindulgence Balance - The ship was very symmetrical from left to right and front to back. Most of the staging happened on the main stage level; however, each level of the ship needed to be constructed to hold at least 15 people. Alignment- Matching the art deco style of the 1920's the alignment was vital in creating the layered tiered look synonymous with the time. Emphasis - The design focused on the center of the stage. This design choice allowed the action to change location within the ship by opening up two sliding set units (located on the main stage level in the ship's center), revealing an open space that allowed other scenic elements to be added or flown in. Lighting was also used to direct the audience's focus and was very effective in changing locations quickly during the production. Movement - Matching the fast-paced antics and action, the movement of the set allowed for quick and easy access to any part of the stage from the off-stage areas. Pattern - Simple shapes were used throughout the design to match the art deco style. The design was based on function and safety. Contrast - The element of the stage that was the most different was the stack at the upstage center section of the design.
