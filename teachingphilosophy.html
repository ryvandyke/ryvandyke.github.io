My Philosophy of Teaching:

Teaching Philosophy

My Philosophy of Teaching begins with the idea that my class is about my students. Good teachers try to connect with their students by developing trust and understanding every day. This process is the best way to engage the student in the learning process. Teaching begins as soon as the students enter the room. I always try to meet the students as they walk into my room. It establishes a positive atmosphere, and it helps me measure how the students feel before I start the lesson for the day.

Over the last twenty-one years, I have developed a series of small summative ways to measure each learner in class. It is not fair to judge students in the classroom based on the highest or lowest skilled student. A good teacher is always looking for ways to measure growth and progress for each student based on their ability and past performances.

Within the Theatre Arts / Media Curriculum, the students are challenged to access and develop their imagination to express themselves creatively. I believe in project-based collaborative learning, where you can allow the student to push themselves in their week areas while also finding and building confidence in the areas and skills they are strong in.

Beyond the standard content, I believe in what Kitt Lavole termed the collaborative world of theatre.

Lavole stated that

In my class, we learn to support and encourage one another.
